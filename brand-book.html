02 — The Mark

Aurora Star

Four ellipse rays
Rotated at 0°, 45°, 90°, 135° — the star cross. Represents the four elements, cardinal directions, and the cyclical nature of cosmic energy.
Sunset wash gradient
Rose #E91E63 → Soft Rose #F06292 → Coral #FF7043, sweeping diagonally. The gradient is shared across all rays, unifying the mark.
Warm white field
#FFF5F0 background. Not pure white — it holds warmth and acts as part of the palette, never just a blank container.
White centre dot
The still point. Ground zero of the star — represents self, presence, the body. Keeps the mark readable at any size.

Clear Space

Minimum clear space
⅕ of the icon's width on all sides. No text, logos, or graphic elements may enter this zone.
Minimum size — digital
28 × 28 px (the white centre dot must remain visible)
Minimum size — print
10 mm / 0.4 in
Corner radius
22% of icon size (Apple / Google store specification)

06 — Usage

Do & Don't

✓
Use the icon with its warm white backgroundThe background is part of the mark. The icon always ships as a square tile — never as a transparent/cut-out on another colour.
✕
Don't place the icon on a dark or coloured backgroundThe mix-blend-mode gradient relies on the warm white field. On dark or coloured surfaces use the full tile, not just the star shape.
✓
Use the Sunset Wash gradient consistentlyRose → Soft Rose → Coral, always diagonal top-left to bottom-right. This direction is fixed — do not flip or rotate the gradient.
✕
Don't recolour the icon or gradientThe aurora star and its gradient are locked. Do not swap colours, apply tints, or use a single-colour version of the icon.
✓
Use Cormorant Garamond for all editorial headlinesDisplay text, marketing copy, App Store descriptions, and the wordmark all use this typeface at weight 300–400.
✕
Don't add drop shadows or effects to the icon tileThe icon has its own elevation through the warm background. External shadows double-dip and look unintentional. Let the tile stand on its own.
✓
Maintain the white centre dot at all sizesAt 20px the dot is the visual anchor that makes the icon legible. Never clip or remove it to "simplify" small-size rendering.
✕
Don't use the icon below 20 × 20 pxBelow this threshold the star structure collapses. Use the wordmark or the Celeste monogram "C" instead.
